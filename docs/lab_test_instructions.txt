CLINIC LAB TEST INSTRUCTIONS
Document ID: LAB-INS-001
Last Updated: January 2026
Applies to: Greenfield Family Health Clinic, Lab and Diagnostic Services

This document describes clinic administrative procedures for common lab tests: how to
prepare logistically, what to bring, and how results are delivered. It does not provide
medical interpretation of results or diagnostic guidance. Result interpretation must always
be discussed with your ordering physician.

1. GENERAL LAB VISIT INSTRUCTIONS
1.1 Bring a valid photo ID and your clinic patient card (or patient portal QR code) to every
    lab visit.
1.2 Lab requisition forms must be issued by a clinic physician before a walk-in sample
    collection, except for the self-pay basic panel described in Section 5.
1.3 Lab Services operates Monday-Saturday 7:00 AM-7:00 PM, and Sunday 8:00 AM-12:00 PM
    for urgent tests only, per the Doctor Schedule document.

2. FASTING BLOOD TESTS (e.g., Fasting Blood Sugar, Lipid Profile)
2.1 Patients should not eat or drink anything except plain water for 8-12 hours before the
    test, as instructed by the ordering physician.
2.2 Routine medications may generally be taken with water unless the physician has
    specifically instructed otherwise; the lab desk cannot advise on medication timing.
2.3 Morning appointments (7:00 AM-10:00 AM) are recommended for fasting tests for
    patient convenience.

3. NON-FASTING BLOOD TESTS (e.g., CBC, Thyroid Panel)
3.1 No fasting is required. Patients may eat and drink normally before the test unless
    otherwise instructed by their physician.

4. URINE AND STOOL SAMPLE COLLECTION
4.1 Sterile collection containers are provided at the lab desk with printed labeling
    instructions.
4.2 Samples collected at home must be delivered to the lab within 2 hours of collection
    and kept at room temperature unless otherwise instructed.
4.3 Female patients should inform the lab technician if a urine test is requested during
    menstruation, as this may affect scheduling but not eligibility for the test.

5. SELF-PAY BASIC PANEL
5.1 Patients may request a basic self-pay panel (CBC, Fasting Blood Sugar, Lipid Profile)
    without a physician requisition, payable at the time of service per the Fee Schedule.
5.2 Self-pay panel results are still recommended to be reviewed with a physician; the lab
    desk cannot interpret results at the time of collection.

6. RESULT DELIVERY
6.1 Routine lab results are typically available within 24-48 hours and are posted to the
    patient portal automatically.
6.2 Urgent/STAT tests ordered by a physician are typically available within 2-4 hours and
    the ordering physician is notified directly.
6.3 Some specialized send-out tests (e.g., certain hormone panels, genetic tests) may take
    5-10 business days; the lab desk will inform the patient of expected turnaround at the
    time of sample collection.
6.4 Results are not given verbally over the phone for privacy reasons. Patients must access
    results via the patient portal or in person with valid ID.

7. RESCHEDULING A LAB VISIT
7.1 Lab appointments tied to a fasting requirement should be rescheduled through the front
    desk or patient portal at least 4 hours in advance, consistent with the clinic's general
    Appointment Policy.

8. IMPORTANT NOTE
8.1 This document covers logistics only. Questions about what a specific test measures,
    what results mean, or whether a test is medically necessary must be directed to a
    licensed physician and are outside the scope of clinic administrative staff or this
    document.

END OF DOCUMENT
