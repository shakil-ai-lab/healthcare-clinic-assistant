CLINIC EMERGENCY POLICY
Document ID: POL-EMR-001
Last Updated: January 2026
Applies to: Greenfield Family Health Clinic

This document describes how Greenfield Family Health Clinic handles emergency situations
during clinic hours and provides guidance on when to seek emergency care elsewhere. This
document is administrative in nature and is not a substitute for professional medical
judgment. If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call your local emergency number
or go to the nearest emergency room immediately rather than waiting for a clinic response.

1. SCOPE OF CLINIC EMERGENCY CAPABILITY
1.1 Greenfield Family Health Clinic is an outpatient primary and specialist care facility.
    It is NOT an emergency room and does not have trauma, ICU, or 24/7 emergency
    department capabilities.
1.2 The clinic can provide initial stabilization and first aid for certain urgent situations
    during clinic operating hours, but serious or life-threatening conditions must be
    directed to a hospital emergency department.

2. SITUATIONS REQUIRING IMMEDIATE EMERGENCY SERVICES (NOT THE CLINIC)
2.1 Examples of situations that require calling emergency services or going directly to an
    emergency room include, but are not limited to: chest pain, difficulty breathing,
    severe bleeding, signs of stroke, loss of consciousness, severe allergic reactions,
    major trauma, suspected poisoning, or any situation the patient perceives as
    life-threatening.
2.2 Clinic staff, including front desk and administrative personnel, are instructed to direct
    any patient describing these symptoms to call emergency services immediately rather
    than waiting for a clinic appointment.

3. DURING CLINIC HOURS
3.1 Patients experiencing an urgent but non-life-threatening issue during clinic hours
    (Monday-Saturday, per posted hours) should inform the front desk immediately upon
    arrival; urgent cases are triaged ahead of the regular queue by clinical staff.
3.2 The clinic maintains basic first-aid and stabilization equipment for use by clinical
    staff while arranging emergency transport if needed.

4. OUTSIDE CLINIC HOURS
4.1 The clinic does not operate an after-hours or on-call emergency line. Patients with
    urgent concerns outside clinic hours should contact emergency services or visit the
    nearest emergency room.
4.2 The patient portal and phone lines are not monitored for emergencies outside posted
    clinic hours.

5. AMBULANCE AND TRANSFER
5.1 If a patient's condition requires transfer to a hospital, clinic staff will call for
    ambulance transport and provide relevant clinic records to the receiving facility with
    patient consent where possible.

6. WHAT THIS DOCUMENT DOES NOT COVER
6.1 This document does not provide medical guidance on diagnosing an emergency, does not
    replace clinical judgment, and must never be used to decide whether a specific
    individual's situation is an emergency. When in doubt, treat the situation as an
    emergency and seek immediate care.
6.2 Any AI assistant, staff member, or automated system referencing this document must
    not attempt to diagnose the severity of a patient's symptoms. If a patient describes
    anything resembling emergency symptoms, the correct response is always to direct them
    to call emergency services or go to the nearest emergency room, without delay or
    further questioning.

END OF DOCUMENT
