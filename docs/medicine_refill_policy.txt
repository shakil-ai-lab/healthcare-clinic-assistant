CLINIC MEDICINE REFILL POLICY
Document ID: POL-REF-001
Last Updated: January 2026
Applies to: Greenfield Family Health Clinic

This document describes the clinic's administrative process for prescription refill requests.
It does not authorize any specific medication, dosage, or refill for any individual patient.
All refill requests remain subject to physician review and approval.

1. HOW TO REQUEST A REFILL
1.1 Refill requests can be submitted through the patient portal, by calling the front desk,
    or by requesting one during a scheduled visit.
1.2 Refill requests should be submitted at least 5 business days before the current
    medication supply runs out to allow time for physician review.
1.3 Each refill request must include the patient's name, date of birth, medication name as
    printed on the prescription label, and the prescribing physician's name.

2. PHYSICIAN REVIEW
2.1 All refill requests are reviewed by the prescribing physician or a covering physician
    from the same department before approval.
2.2 The clinic cannot guarantee same-day review; standard review time is 1-3 business days.
2.3 Refills for controlled substances (e.g., certain pain medications, sedatives, stimulants)
    require an in-person or telehealth follow-up visit before approval, regardless of how
    recently the patient was last seen, per clinic and regulatory policy.
2.4 Refills for chronic condition medications (e.g., blood pressure, diabetes, thyroid
    medications) are generally reviewed more quickly if the patient has had a routine
    follow-up visit within the last 6 months.

3. WHEN A FOLLOW-UP VISIT IS REQUIRED
3.1 A follow-up visit is required before a refill will be approved if:
    - The patient has not been seen for that condition in over 6 months, or
    - The medication is a controlled substance, or
    - The physician has flagged the prescription as requiring periodic monitoring
      (e.g., lab work tied to the medication).
3.2 If a follow-up visit is required, the front desk will contact the patient to schedule one
    per the Appointment Policy.

4. PHARMACY COORDINATION
4.1 Approved refills are sent electronically to the patient's pharmacy of record, or made
    available for pickup at the clinic pharmacy counter if applicable.
4.2 Patients wishing to change their pharmacy of record must update this through the
    patient portal or front desk before submitting a refill request.

5. DENIED REFILL REQUESTS
5.1 If a refill is denied, the front desk will notify the patient and provide the reason
    given by the physician (e.g., follow-up required, medication discontinued, dosage
    under review).
5.2 Denied refill requests do not constitute medical advice regarding alternative treatment;
    patients must discuss alternatives directly with their physician.

6. EMERGENCY MEDICATION SHORTAGES
6.1 Patients who run out of a critical medication before a refill is processed should
    contact the clinic directly rather than waiting for the standard review queue; the front
    desk will flag these as priority requests.
6.2 This document does not cover what to do in a medical emergency; refer to the
    Emergency Policy document for emergency situations.

7. IMPORTANT NOTE
7.1 Clinic administrative staff cannot approve, deny, or adjust medication dosages. All
    clinical decisions about medication are made exclusively by a licensed physician.

END OF DOCUMENT
