CLINIC APPOINTMENT POLICY
Document ID: POL-APT-001
Last Updated: January 2026
Applies to: Greenfield Family Health Clinic, all departments

1. BOOKING AN APPOINTMENT
1.1 Appointments can be booked through the clinic's front desk (Mon-Sat, 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM),
    via the patient portal (24/7), or by phone at the main clinic line.
1.2 New patients must complete a registration form at least 24 hours before their first
    appointment, either online or in person.
1.3 Same-day appointments are available on a limited basis for General Medicine and are
    subject to physician availability. Same-day slots open at 8:00 AM each day.
1.4 Specialist appointments (Cardiology, Dermatology, Orthopedics, Pediatrics, ENT) require
    a referral from a General Medicine physician unless the patient is a returning specialist
    patient within the last 12 months.

2. APPOINTMENT TYPES
2.1 New Patient Consultation - 30 minutes
2.2 Follow-up Visit - 15 minutes
2.3 Specialist Consultation - 30-45 minutes depending on department
2.4 Annual Physical Examination - 45 minutes
2.5 Minor Procedure Visit - 30-60 minutes depending on procedure
2.6 Telehealth Consultation - 20 minutes (available for follow-ups only, not new patients)

3. RESCHEDULING AND CANCELLATION
3.1 Patients may reschedule or cancel an appointment free of charge up to 4 hours before
    the scheduled time.
3.2 Cancellations made less than 4 hours before the appointment, or no-shows, may incur a
    cancellation fee as outlined in the Fee Schedule document.
3.3 Patients with three or more consecutive no-shows within a 12-month period may be
    required to prepay for future appointments.
3.4 To reschedule, patients should use the patient portal or call the front desk. Rescheduling
    requests are not accepted via email for security reasons.

4. LATE ARRIVALS
4.1 Patients arriving more than 15 minutes late for a standard appointment may be asked to
    reschedule to avoid delaying subsequent patients.
4.2 Patients arriving late for a same-day slot may lose their reserved slot if another patient
    is available to be seen.

5. WAITLIST
5.1 Patients may join a waitlist for earlier openings with a specific physician. Waitlist
    notifications are sent automatically via SMS or the patient portal when a slot opens.
5.2 Waitlist offers must be confirmed within 30 minutes or the slot is offered to the next
    patient on the list.

6. WALK-INS
6.1 Walk-in patients are accepted for General Medicine only and are seen after all
    scheduled patients, based on availability and triage priority.
6.2 Walk-ins requiring urgent attention are triaged by a nurse and may be seen ahead of
    the queue based on clinical urgency, not arrival order.

7. ACCOMPANYING VISITORS
7.1 Patients may bring one accompanying adult to appointments. Additional visitors may be
    asked to wait in the reception area due to space constraints.
7.2 Pediatric patients must be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian at all times.

8. CONTACT FOR APPOINTMENT ISSUES
8.1 For appointment-related questions not covered in this document, patients should contact
    the front desk directly. This document does not cover clinical or diagnostic questions,
    which must be addressed by a licensed physician during a consultation.

END OF DOCUMENT
